Gado-Gado: Indonesian Vegetable Salad with Creamy Peanut Sauce and Tempeh

Gado-Gado, an iconic Indonesian dish, is a vibrant and hearty vegetable salad that celebrates the diverse flavors of Indonesia. This beloved street food is a harmonious mix of boiled vegetables, such as spinach, cabbage, and bean sprouts, alongside crunchy lontong (rice cake), crispy fried tofu and tempeh, and a hard-boiled egg. The star of the dish is the creamy, slightly sweet peanut sauce, which is generously drizzled over the colorful medley, creating a delightful fusion of textures and tastes. Often enjoyed as a main course, Gado-Gado is not only a nutritious option but also a feast for the senses.

In addition to its delicious flavors, Gado-Gado holds cultural significance as a symbol of Indonesia's rich culinary heritage. Whether served in local warungs (small restaurants) or at festive gatherings, Gado-Gado is a versatile dish that can be tailored to suit different preferences, making it a beloved staple in Indonesian homes. Experience the true essence of Indonesian cuisine with each bite of Gado-Gado, where freshness, flavor, and tradition come together in perfect harmony.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup spinach, boiled
  • 1 cup cabbage, boiled
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts, blanched
  • 1/2 cup lontong (rice cake), cubed
  • 1/2 cup fried tofu, cubed
  • 1/2 cup fried tempeh, cubed
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, sliced
  • 1 small potato, boiled and sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/4 cup peanuts, roasted and ground
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind juice
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup water

Steps

  1. Boil the spinach, cabbage, and potato until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. Blanch the bean sprouts in boiling water for about 1 minute. Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the tofu and tempeh until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  4. Slice the hard-boiled egg, cucumber, and boiled potato. Set aside.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the ground peanuts, minced garlic, and sliced red chili.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix the tamarind juice, palm sugar, sweet soy sauce, salt, and water until well combined.
  7. Add the peanut mixture to the tamarind mixture and stir until smooth, creating the peanut sauce.
  8. Arrange the boiled vegetables, bean sprouts, lontong, fried tofu, fried tempeh, hard-boiled egg, potato, and cucumber on a serving plate.
  9. Drizzle the peanut sauce generously over the arranged ingredients.
  10. Toss gently to mix all the ingredients with the sauce.
  11. Serve immediately and enjoy your Gado-Gado!
